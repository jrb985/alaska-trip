## Two things that shaped this trip

**The original goal was Nome — but you cannot ride to Nome.** It has no road connection to the
North American highway system at all. It's a fly-in / barge-in town, with an isolated local gravel
network that dead-ends into tundra. Deadhorse became the destination instead: road-continuous from
Seattle, and genuinely the end of the road.

**The hardest constraint is fuel, not weather.** Coldfoot → Deadhorse is **239 miles with no
fuel** — the longest fuel gap on any public road in the United States. The F650GS carries 4.4
gallons, about 230 miles claimed and closer to 200 loaded. It is *short*. And the fix isn't a
bigger tank: on this bike the tank lives **under the seat**, inside the frame, so no long-range
tank exists. The answer is a Rotopax strapped to the rack.
